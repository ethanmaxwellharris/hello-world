Reflection on 30 minutes of reading, "Ten arguments for deleting your social media accounts right now"

Jaron Lanier's book, "Ten arguments for deleting your social media accounts right now" opens by engaging the reader with cats, a trope that pervades the Internet to no end. In fact, if you follow the CSS lessons on freecodecamp.com you'll find a pleasant kitten'y surprise for you there. Lanier, who is a Silicon Valleite himself, urges the reader to consider the consequences of engagement and the social media monster that has ravaged through the ego the past decade. He starts his quest by educating the reader about the behavior modification techniques, and what they are, which seek to keep you addicted to the activity you're focused on. I couldn't help but think about the game, Harry Potter Puzzles & Spells since I've been somewhat "engaged" with it over the past few weeks. I've always taken the "high road" and avoided such trivial and "brain dead" games of it's ilk such as Candy Crush. But darnit, they try so hard to suck me in. Between the shiny bits, the explosive ending when you successfully finish a puzzle, points flying around the screen to their respective places and the overlay of nostalgia inducing thematics from the Harry Potter world I find it hard to avoid. Oh! A notification just let me know that a new event has started. So when Lanier points out that I've been B.F. Skinner'd into playing this game repeatedly I can't help but feel a sense of nihilistic dread as I wonder about what other algorithmic-induced horrors are lurking in my subconscious waiting to influence me at a time of weakness. I refuse to buy gold coins to replenish my hearts and keep playing Zynga, please stop. I bet you can think of some other platform that has behaviorally modified your commodity of a brain into using it constantly. Instagram gives you hearts, Facebook grants you likes, and Reddit gives you a platform to feel smug as you downvote those who disagree with your poorly formed opinion. No matter the platform, you're being tracked, monitored, and subjugated to stress and feedback for the purpose of "growth" on their end and "engagement" on yours. We all should know this by now, I'm interested to see how Lanier proposes we "fight back" per se. It's been some time since I've actively participated in social media, with the exception of Reddit which is simply an "anti-social-media social media", but I look forward to the remainder of my reads.

A Final Word


  • Ginger Soda

    Ginger Soda, also known as Ginger Ale, is a carbonated soft drink flavoured with ginger. On principle it is to be regarded as detestable, detractable, and depressing as a beverage choice.

  • Hand Sanitizer

    Hand Sanitizer, containing at least 75% alcohol, should have a Bacteriostatic Rate of 99.9%. Anything less is worthy of the utmost contempt. Once I read a package that said, "Kills up to 99.9% of most bacteria" As if they're going to slip it past us as the consumer that some Escherichia or Pseudomonas made it past the bouncer because they're cute.

  • Penang Curry

    Penang Curry, see also "phanaeng", is a Thai curry which is sweet and salty with a nutty peanut flavor. The best Penang's are at the mercy of their restuarant under the following condition: they serve an appropriate portion of rice to not exceed 2 cups, the protein is sliced (not diced or shredded), the extra filligree, aka vegetables, are steamed not fried, and the restuarant has a Spice Caddy with a vinegar based spice, fish sauce based spice, chili paste, and chili flakes. Oh my, just thinking of it makes my mouth water. Do more of this.

    Note: not all resturants are the same, there are offenders amongst us who not only lack a Spice Caddy but look at you in confusion when you ask for one. Sriracha is NOT an appropriate substitute...

  • Sales; 1/2 Off

    If you're having a sale and you declare boldy on your sinage, "Buy One Get One Free!" Don't ring up the most expensive item first at 1/2 price, then the second item at 1/2 price. That's "Buy Two Items, The Most Expensive of Which Will be the Basis of the Price, Wherein the Least Expensive of Which Will be the Remainder of the Price, Afterwhich Both Items will be Priced at 50%" This is what I call, "false advertising" and is infuriating.

  • Salutations

    Salutations are a necessary part of interaction. For every beginning there must be an end. You open and you close. Such is the way of life. Therefore, do not leave open ended converstaions in the wake of your social fluttering abouts. Say goodbye, goodnight, au revoir, konbonwa, and auf wiedersehen.
